History[edit]
Historically a town of British India, it was founded in the mid-eighteenth century by a local leader named Thakur bhoop singh, who also built a fort in the area, which was dismantled after the Indian Rebellion of 1857[2].
In 1901, Rehar had a population of 3000 [3].

Demographics[edit]
As of 2001 India census,[4] Rehar had a population of 4,954. Males constitute 53% of the population and females 47%. Rehar has an average literacy rate of 39%, lower than the national average of 59.5%; with 59% of the males and 41% of females literate. 17% of the population is under 6 years of age.[1]

Mosque[edit]
There are seven mosques (Maszid) in Rehar town. All are very old and located on every main street. Out of seven mosques there are three which run Three Madarsas in the town for the free study of Quran and Hadith. One mosque is called Jama Maszid which is larger than the other mosques of town.

Temple[edit]
There is a lone temple (Shivalaya) dated back to 1900. The temple used to be inside the residential premises of the late Pandit Charan Dass, father of Pandit Musaddi Lal Kaushik, who later settled in Dhampur. In the remaining portion of residence of Pt Charan Dass and permitted  by Pandit Musaddi  Lal Kaushik, now a  primary school is being run by the locals through Government aid.[citation needed]

Mona Sati Math[edit]
A Small Math belongs to Sati Mona the Ancestor Sati said to be approximately  400 years old is situated in the field owned by a local Brahmin Farmer on the out skirt of Rehar on National High way Number 74.

Education institutions[edit]
Inter college[edit]
Govt Navratan Inter College (G.N.I.C): which has been built on land donated by Kunwar Nav Ratan Singh Rathod and Pandit Musaddi Lal Kaushik s/o Pandit Charan Dass Kaushik, is imparting education not only to the local residents but catering to the educational needs of common men of a number of villages in the vicinity.

A P.G.Degree college is situated about 2 Kilometers away at Baadigarh Riyaasat of Kunwar Shri Tripurari Singh Rathore Estate Rehar also a P.G. college is approximately eight kilometers away at [Afzalgarh].

Hospital[edit]
The village has a government hospital specially for children and maternity care. Currently Nurse Malo Devi is appointed as head of the hospital, She is working there since last 30 years. The hospital has the facility of normal child delivery and Oxygen cylinder. The delivery services in hospital is available 24x7.
