The Chulanur Peafowl Sanctuary is in Palakkad district of Central Kerala in India.


Understand[edit]  Chulanur Peafowl SanctuaryThe Chulanur Peafowl (Peacocks) Sancturary is located in the districts of Thrissur and Palakkad. The sanctuary headquarters is at Chulanur at about 8 km from Thiruvilwamala. Chulanur Peafowl Sanctuary is dedicated to the memory of a leading Indian ornithologist, K.K. Neelakantan.
History[edit]This sanctuary was established in 14 March 1996. It is the only peacock sanctuary in Kerala. 
Landscape[edit]The terrain is undulating with small hillocks and valleys, with occasional rocky outcrops and patches. The average altitude is about 120 m.
  Rocky patches of Chulanur Peafowl SanctuaryFlora and fauna[edit]Climate[edit]The area receives both southwest and northeast monsoons. The average annual percipation is 2200 mm. Dry conditions prevail in the area in summer months due to the hot winds coming through the Palakkad gap.
