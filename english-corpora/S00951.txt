Before We Ruled the Earth is a two-part documentary television miniseries that premiered on February 9, 2003 on the Discovery Channel. The program featured early human history and the challenges human beings faced thousands of years ago. It also features animals examples such as: Woolly mammoth, Megantereon, American buffalo, cave bear, and Irish elk (Megaloceros giganteus). The first episode was called "Hunt or Be Hunted" and the second called "Mastering the Beasts."

Episodes[edit]
Episode 1[edit]
"Hunt or Be Hunted" (narrated by Linda Hunt[1]). The first part in the series shows how humans started out as prey for other animals such as saber-toothed cats. It depicts a saber-toothed cat killing an antelope and leaving the remains of the carcass; our ancestors attempt to steal the remains from the cat and one of the group is killed as the saber tooth spots them. It also shows how we discovered tools and made use of the sharpened stone. Later the show showed how as we grew more intelligent, that we were able to turn the tables on the beasts.[clarification needed]
Species discussed:
Homo ergaster,
Megantereon,
Homo erectus,
Giant elk,
Neanderthal,
Steppe bison, and
Human (Cro-Magnon).

Episode 2[edit]
"Mastering the Beasts" (narrated by John Slattery[1]). The second and last part in the series takes place during the Ice Age. It shows how the Cro-Magnon and modern humans have become the dominant species and how we hunted animals such as mammoth, Irish elk, aurochs and buffalo. Threats however include giant cave bears and wolves. Death on the tundra was common and to survive our ancestors need to be able to adapt to change, such as the warming of the earth at the end of the last Ice Age ... something most of the giant beasts could not do, and therefore suffered extinction.
Species discussed:
Human (Cro-Magnon and Paleo-Indian),
Cave bear,
Aurochs,
Woolly mammoth,
Tundra wolf,
Megalonyx jeffersoni, and
American buffalo.
