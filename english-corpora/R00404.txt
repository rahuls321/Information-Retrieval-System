Kuyeda (Russian: Куеда) is a rural locality (a settlement) and the administrative center of Kuyedinsky District of Perm Krai, Russia. Population: 9,551 (2010 Census); 9,809 (2002 Census); 9,613 (1989 Census).


== History ==
The settlement started in 1915 as a railway station during the construction of the Kazan–Yekaterinburg line. It was named after a nearby village which, in turn, got its name from the Kuyeda River.
